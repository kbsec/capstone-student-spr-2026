Report ID:      MDG-2024-0419
Classification: UNCLASSIFIED
Date:           19 April 2024
Author:         Threat Analysis Center

Threat Assessment — APT Activity Q1

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Quarterly review of Advanced Persistent Threat (APT) activity
targeting the defense industrial base during Q1 2024, based on
unclassified threat feeds, vendor advisories, and partner sharing.

KEY FINDINGS

1. Spear-phishing campaigns targeting defense contractors increased
   23% compared to Q4 2023.  Primary lure themes: budget proposals,
   contract announcements, and conference invitations.

2. Two new malware families were identified in public reporting
   that specifically target Linux-based embedded systems.  Neither
   has been observed in campaigns against our sector.

3. Supply chain attacks against open-source software repositories
   remained at elevated levels.  Seven confirmed compromised
   packages were identified in public repositories during Q1.

RECOMMENDATIONS

- Update email filtering rules to block newly identified lure domains
- Verify no instances of compromised packages in development environments
- Conduct refresher training on spear-phishing identification
